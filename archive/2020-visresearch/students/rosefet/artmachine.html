The aim of this machine is to create art with minimal human interference.

Below shows the process that lead to the final machine.

This is the first machine.

It has a simple mechanism, where turning the handle causes the piece of charcoal to move in a cicular pattern.

This is the second machine.

Turning the handle causes the large inner plate to move, as well as the disk holding the arm.

The ridges of the corrugated cardboard did not work well so an alternative was sought.

This is the outcome of the above machine.

This is the final machine.

The wood construction is more sturdy than the cardboard, and the large rubber band works better than the 'gears' of the corrugated cardboard.

Above shows the 'art' created using the final machine.
